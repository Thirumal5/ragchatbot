Fever:
Fever is a temporary rise in body temperature, often due to infection.
Drink plenty of fluids, rest well, and monitor temperature.
If fever lasts more than 3 days or becomes very high, consult a doctor.

Common Cold:
Common cold is caused by viral infection.
Symptoms include runny nose, sneezing, mild fever, and sore throat.
Rest, hydration, and warm fluids may help recovery.

Headache:
Headaches can occur due to stress, dehydration, lack of sleep, or illness.
Drinking water, rest, and relaxation may reduce symptoms.
Persistent or severe headaches require medical advice.

Stomach Pain:
Stomach pain can be caused by indigestion, infection, or food intolerance.
Light food, hydration, and rest may help.
If pain is severe or continuous, consult a doctor.

Dehydration:
Occurs when body loses more fluids than it takes in.
Symptoms include thirst, dry mouth, weakness, and dizziness.
Drink water, ORS, or fluids regularly.

Fatigue:
Fatigue means feeling tired or lacking energy.
Common causes include stress, poor sleep, or illness.
Balanced diet, proper sleep, and hydration help recovery.

Cough:
Cough may result from cold, infection, or allergies.
Warm fluids and rest may help.
Persistent cough should be checked by a doctor.

General Health Advice:
Maintain balanced diet, regular exercise, good sleep, and hydration.
Avoid self-medication without professional advice.
Consult healthcare professionals for serious symptoms.
